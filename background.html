Summary of Our Analysis

A comparative analysis was performed by Wang et al. to observe the difference between RNA-sequencing (RNA-seq) and microarray in the detection of differentially expressed genes (DEGs). In their study, they used 105 rat livers that were exposed to 27 drugs with different modes of action to identify differentially expressed levels of genes. In our study, we tried to replicate the same analysis using one of the drugs they they analysed in their study to see whether we could achieve the similar results.

Of the 27 drugs, we decided to choose the drug gemfibrozil (GEM) because its mode of action, PPARA, demonstrated a modest gene expression predictability. Furthermore, when quantitative polymerase chain reaction (qPCR) was used to confirm the results of 18-cancer related genes, it was found that those rats subjected to GEM, compared to the other test drug sets, had not expressed a below-median cancer gene. For our controls, we used the two matched controls that was run over the same 7-day duration as GEM.

Prior to beginning the analysis, we performed a quality check on the paired-end sequences we obtained from National Centre for Biotechnology Information’s sequence read archive1 on Wang et al.’s study. We used the tool FastQC2 to assess which reads needed to be trimmed or excluded from the study.

We decided to use two pipelines to carry our analysis: Pipeline 5 (P5): University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC)3-TopHat4 -Cufflinks5 and Pipeline 6 (P6): Ensembl6-Bowtie 7-RNA-seq by Expectation Maximisation (RSEM)8. We chose these two pipelines to determine whether using two different gene models and two different approaches to mapping and quantifying transcripts could produce similar results. Both pipelines were carried out using the same rat genome “Rn4” that Wang et al. had used in their study and on the latest rat genome “Rn6”.

P5’s TopHat and P6’s Bowtie were tools used to align mapped reads to the genome before proceeding to the quantification of transcripts. Using the webserver, users could view where these reads were mapped to genome using the UCSC browser3.

One of the obvious differences observed between the two pipelines was how the expression levels of genes were quantified. Cufflinks output values were in the Fragments Per Kilobase of exon per Million fragments mapped (FPKM) units, while RSEM gave raw counts. The difference between FPKM and counts was that FPKM outputs were already normalised according to the transcript length, while raw counts had to still be normalised using statistical methods.

The resulting genes identified from these two pipelines were put through the statistical tools, LIMMA9 and edgeR10, for P5 and P6, respectively. The log fold change (FC) and p-value for each gene was calculated. The gene was determined to be significantly expressed when the absolute logFC was >1.5 and the p.value was <0.05. These results could then be visualised using a volcano plot using the R package 12. A added feature to this volcano was added so that a user could select the number of top DEGs they wanted to view.
